Interactive Tutorial

Step 1: Understanding Quantum Numbers

The hydrogen atom's electron is described by three quantum numbers:

  • n (Principal): Determines energy and size
  • l (Angular Momentum): Determines shape
  • m (Magnetic): Determines orientation

Step 2: Energy Levels

Energy in hydrogen depends only on n: E = -13.6/n¬≤ eV

  • n=1: Ground state (-13.6 eV)
  • n=2: First excited state (-3.4 eV)
  • Higher n: Higher energy, larger orbitals

Step 3: Orbital Shapes

Different l values create different orbital shapes:

  • s orbitals (l=0): Spherical
  • p orbitals (l=1): Dumbbell-shaped
  • d orbitals (l=2): Complex, multi-lobed

Step 4: Probability Density

The visualization shows where the electron is most likely to be found:

  • Brighter regions = higher probability
  • Darker regions = lower probability
  • Nodes = zero probability
